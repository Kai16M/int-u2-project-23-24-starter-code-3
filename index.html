What Is Bullying?

Bullying is the use of force, coercion, hurtful teasing or threat, to abuse, aggressively dominate or intimidate. The behavior is often repeated and habitual. One essential prerequisite is the perception of an imbalance of physical or social power. This imbalance distinguishes bullying from conflict. Bullying is a subcategory of aggressive behavior characterized by hostile intent, imbalance of power and repetition over a period of time.

Why Does It Happen?

Wanting to dominate others and improve their social status Having low self-esteem and wanting to feel better about themselves Having a lack of remorse or failing to recognise their behaviour as a problem, feeling angry, frustrated or jealous, struggling socially feeling powerless suffering from insecurity needing to control others enjoying the rewards they get from bullying

How To Prevent It From Happening

Ways to prevent, stop, or deter bullying is by reporting the bullying you see to an adult or online administrator. You also can go to Stop Bullying and find out what other ways you can stop bullying in your own school enviornment.
